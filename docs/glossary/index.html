Glossary

Pricing#

We check each payment gateway's website on a regular basis and list their advertised rates here. We do our best to accurately and fairly represent pricing and include a link to the gateway's pricing page under "References" to allow you to validate this data. Pricing is that for the country selected.

To process a card payment, the payment gateway incurs a "interchange" fee set by the card schemes (eg Visa / Mastercard), there are hundreds of different rates depending on the type of card used and transaction type, ranging from 0% to over 4%.

There are generally two ways a payment gateway will charge you:

  • Interchange ++: eg $0.20 + Interchange + % Fee: This means $0.20 + the interchange fee marked up by an amount (eg 0.5%)
  • Blended rate: eg $0.20 + 1.8%: This means $0.20 + 1.8% regardless of the interchange fee, this makes your costs more predictable, but will generally cost you more, especially if the bulk of your volume is for low cost transaction types (eg. consumer debit cards).

Furthermore, some payment gateways are not "merchant acquirers" and do not bundle what's called a "merchant account", these are described in the pricing matrix as "Gateway Only". These will typically only have a fixed fee per transaction, eg $0.20 however you will require an additional service called a "merchant account" which is where you will need to pay interchange + markup.

The pricing matrix includes the following data:

FieldDescription
Monthly FeeIf a monthly subscription fee is payable. Some payment providers have several "plans" which are listed separated by commas.
ProcessingThe cost to process an ecommerce transaction. Additional charges may apply, such as for MOTO transactions, or transactions that dont take advantage of anti-fraud facilities provided by the gateway.
InterationalAn additional cost (on top of the processing fee) for using an international card. For example using a European card in the USA or vice versa.
POSThe cost to process a customer-present (point of sale) transaction. You will typically need to swipe, chip and pin, or use contactless to receive this rate.
Activation FeeA one time cost to activate an account.
Termination FeeA one time cost to terminate an account.
ForexA fee (typically over the mid-market exchange rate) to convert the payment currency into the settlement currency.
Bespoke PricingThe minimum monthly transaction volume required before the gateway will consider giving you a rate other than their "list price".

Note:

  • Where see website is listed, we do not have recent data for the selected country and you may be able to find further information on the website. Use the link in the "References" section.
  • Where on application is listed, the payment provider does not advertise their rates (or has bespoke rates), and you need to speak to them directly. In general we know the kind of rates you will be offered, but it is not public information we are allowed to publish.

PCI DSS#

PCI DSS is a set of security standards required by all businesses that store or transmit cardholder data. Merchant and software vendors must also be compliant, even if that compliance is "we dont touch cardholder data", this is documented via an AOC (attestation of compliance) or SAQ (self assessment questionaire). It is your responsibility to ensure all vendors and payment processors you use are PCI DSS compliant (requirement 12.8.4).

Acceptable sources of documentation are:

  • VISA's online registry here
  • An AOC document (service providers transacting over 300,000 transactions p/a)
  • An SAQ document (service providers transacting less than 300,000 transactions p/a)
